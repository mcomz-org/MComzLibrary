# Martin's Water Engineering Bibliography

Source: Martin Currie (martin@andeye.com), water engineer and MComzOS co-founder.

7 Zotero collections, ~150 items. Initial assessment completed 2026-04-10.

## Assessment Summary

Martin believes most of his library isn't optimal for non-water professionals. While this is true for the commercial textbooks, several categories contain resources specifically designed for non-specialists and are highly relevant to Sustainable Survival.

### Highest-value candidates (licence verification needed)

| Source | Items | Likely licence | Why valuable |
|---|---|---|---|
| **WHO publications** | GDWQ, Wastewater Guidelines (4 vols), Sanitation Safety Planning | CC BY-NC-SA 3.0 IGO | Definitive global standards, written for policy makers and field workers, not just engineers. NC = excluded from core, recommended as addition. |
| **Sphere Handbook** (4th ed.) | 1 | Verify at spherestandards.org | THE humanitarian standards reference. Covers WASH, shelter, food, health. Used by every humanitarian organisation. |
| **Oxfam WASH Technical Briefing Notes** | ~30 PDFs | Verify with Oxfam | Practical field guides written for aid workers with no engineering background. Topics: hand-dug wells, spring protection, water quality testing, household water treatment, drainage, sanitation, desalination. These are exactly the kind of resource MComzOS needs. |
| **WEDC (Loughborough) resources** | Several | Often CC-licensed, verify | Poster 004 "F diagram" is a classic public health teaching tool. |

### Out of scope for MComzOS library

- Water Climate Discussion papers (Martin's own research with andeye) — policy-focused, not practical survival
- Academic journal articles — too technical
- SUEZ Handbook of Industrial Water Treatment — commercial/industrial focus
- Australian water recycling guidelines — regional regulatory documents
