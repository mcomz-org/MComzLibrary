# Deep Research Task: Gap Analysis & Licence Verification

## Context

You are researching content for the MComzOS offline library. Read `docs/RATIONALE.md` first for full context.

**MComzOS** is an emergency communications OS. The library supports **Sustainable Survival**: *the ability for you and your family to thrive without impacting the ability of others to thrive, now or in the future.*

**Selection criteria:** Public domain or non-NC open licence. Practical. Accessible language. Minimal overlap with existing content. No combat/weapons content.

**NC items are excluded from core ZIMs** to keep the library freely shareable. NC items are listed as recommended additions.

## Research Tasks

### Task 1: Find missing texts

For each category below, search for candidate texts. For each candidate, report: title, author, year, licence (with source URL for the licence claim), estimated size, and a 1-sentence assessment of practical value.

**Water and sanitation:**
- WHO Water Safety Plan manuals — what licence?
- Sphere Handbook (4th ed., 2018) — what are the exact redistribution terms on spherestandards.org?
- Oxfam WASH Technical Briefing Notes — what are Oxfam's redistribution terms for their free PDFs?
- CDC and EPA water treatment guides — these should be US Gov PD, find the best ones
- Any other PD water purification or field sanitation manuals

**Agriculture and food production:**
- USDA extension service publications on seed saving, food preservation, basic farming (US Gov = PD)
- Any PD or CC BY/CC BY-SA permaculture or small-scale farming guides
- FAO publications — what licence does FAO use?

**Non-Western literature (readability is critical):**
- Evaluate these specific PD translations for modern readability:
  - Panchatantra — which PD English translation is most readable?
  - Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam (Edward FitzGerald, 1859) — is this accessible or archaic?
  - Journey to the West — is there any readable PD English translation, or are they all abridged/academic?
  - Tale of Genji (Arthur Waley translation) — is this engaging or impenetrable?
  - Any other non-Western classics with good PD English translations
- For each: "Would a general reader picking this up at random enjoy it, or would it put them off trying the next book?"

**Civilian first aid:**
- Are any Red Cross/Red Crescent first aid manuals available under open licences?
- St John Ambulance guides — what licence?
- US government first aid manuals (military ones excluded unless purely medical)
- Any OGL first aid guides from UK government?

**Modern CC-licensed literature:**
- Search Unglue.it for fiction released under CC0, CC BY, or CC BY-SA
- Search Standard Ebooks for any modern (post-1950) works
- Quality bar: would a general reader enjoy this as much as Sherlock Holmes or Pride and Prejudice?

### Task 5: Overlap analysis

For each text currently in the library, note in one sentence what unique content it provides. Flag any items where 80%+ of their practical content duplicates another included text. Recommend removals or consolidations.
