# MComz Survival Reference

*Practical reference for communities without access to conventional infrastructure — click any title to open the document.*

Medical, mental health, shelter, navigation, civil defence, communications, engineering, and water & sanitation. Civilian-focused — there is nothing here on combat, evasion, or weapons.

> **Medical disclaimer.** These texts are informational resources for austere environments. They do not replace professional medical care. **If professional help can be reached — emergency services, a doctor, a hospital — call them first.** These texts are for when it cannot. Users must follow local laws regarding medical practice. Never forage mushrooms without training from a local expert.

## Triage — what to reach for first

In an emergency, work down this list. Each item depends on the one before it — do not skip ahead. Most problems below are addressed by more than one document; the first link on each line is the fastest useful reference, with deeper references following it.

### Minutes — immediate life threats

1. **Scene safety.** Fire, smoke, toxic gas, unbreathable atmosphere, structural collapse, live electricity, flooding, hostile people — move yourself and the casualty out before anything else. You cannot help someone if you become the next casualty.
   → [CERT Basic Training](fema-cert-basic-training.pdf) · [Are You Ready?](fema-are-you-ready.pdf)

2. **Airway and breathing.** Casualty unresponsive and not breathing: begin chest compressions immediately — hands-only CPR is better than none, even if you have no training. Choking: back blows, then abdominal thrusts. Smoke or toxic-gas inhalation: fresh air, keep the casualty calm, monitor breathing.
   → Quick card: [HSE INDG347](hse-indg347-first-aid.pdf) · Illustrated procedure: [FM 4-25.11 First Aid](fm-4-25-11-first-aid.pdf)

3. **Life-threatening bleeding.** For catastrophic bleeding from a limb — blood spurting, pooling, or soaking through dressings — apply a tourniquet **as the first action**, high and tight on the limb, 5–8 cm above the wound, or as high as possible if the wound is at the upper thigh, shoulder, or if its location is unclear. Tighten until bleeding stops; note the time of application. Once placed, do not loosen. For less severe bleeding, firm direct pressure on the wound with a clean cloth or dressing. For bleeding from the neck, armpit, or groin where a tourniquet cannot work, pack the wound firmly with gauze or clean cloth and press hard.
   → [HSE INDG347](hse-indg347-first-aid.pdf) · [FM 4-25.11 First Aid](fm-4-25-11-first-aid.pdf) · trained medics: [SOF Medical Handbook](sof-medical-handbook.pdf)
   *Note: FM 4-25.11 dates from 2002 and presents tourniquets as a last resort. Current evidence-based doctrine (Stop the Bleed, TCCC) treats early tourniquet application as first-line for severe limb bleeding. Follow the guidance above; use FM 4-25.11 for its illustrated procedures.*

4. **Shock, spinal injury, burns, fractures, poisoning.** Assess and stabilise — the procedures differ by injury and need an illustrated reference.
   → [FM 4-25.11 First Aid](fm-4-25-11-first-aid.pdf)

### Hours to days — short-term survival

5. **Shelter from the elements.** In cold, wet, or extreme heat, shelter matters before water and far before food. Hypothermia kills in hours; so does heat stroke. Build or find shelter now.
   → [FM 21-76 Survival](fm-21-76-survival.pdf) (shelter chapter) · Biome-specific (jungle, desert, arctic, maritime): [AFH 10-644](afh-10-644-survival.pdf)

6. **Water.** Roughly 2–3 litres per person per day. Find a source, then make it safe before drinking.
   → Disinfection quick reference: [EPA Emergency Disinfection](epa-emergency-disinfection.pdf) · Finding water: [FM 21-76 Survival](fm-21-76-survival.pdf) · Urban water-supply disruption: [Are You Ready?](fema-are-you-ready.pdf)

7. **Location and signalling.** If you are lost: where are you, where are you going, can you raise help? Map and compass first; radio if you have one.
   → [FM 3-25.26 Map Reading and Land Navigation](fm-3-25-26-map-reading.pdf) · [MCRP 3-40.3C Antenna Handbook](mcrp-3-40-3c-antenna-handbook.pdf)

8. **Food.** Humans survive weeks without food. Do not risk unfamiliar plants, animals, or water for a meal if you are otherwise stable — address everything above first.
   → [FM 21-76 Survival](fm-21-76-survival.pdf) · [AFH 10-644](afh-10-644-survival.pdf)

### Weeks onwards — sustained survival

9. **Prolonged medical care without a doctor.** Ongoing nursing, wound care, communicable disease, mental health, childbirth. This is the territory of the *Ship's Medicine Chest*, written precisely for non-doctors weeks from port.
   → [The Ship's Medicine Chest](ships-medicine-chest.pdf) · trained medics: [SOF Medical Handbook](sof-medical-handbook.pdf)

10. **Sanitation and disease prevention.** In prolonged emergencies, disease kills more people than the event itself. Separate drinking water from waste, keep food covered, wash hands with soap or ash and water, manage human waste at least 30 m from any water source.
    → [Are You Ready?](fema-are-you-ready.pdf) · drinking-water disinfection: [EPA Emergency Disinfection](epa-emergency-disinfection.pdf)

11. **Community response.** Organising multi-casualty triage, search-and-rescue, shared work and watches.
    → [CERT Basic Training](fema-cert-basic-training.pdf)

12. **Rebuilding and sustaining infrastructure.** Load-bearing knots and rigging, basic machines, electricity and batteries.
    → [Boatswain's Mate Manual](boatswains-mate-manual.pdf) · [Basic Machines and How They Work](basic-machines-navy.pdf) · NEETS electricity modules [1](neets-module-01.pdf), [2](neets-module-02.pdf), [3](neets-module-03.pdf), [4](neets-module-04.pdf), [18](neets-module-18.pdf)

## What we hope to do

- **Regional foraging collections.** Foraging is highly region-specific — a plant safe in Scotland may have a toxic lookalike in Australia. We plan small regional collections (delivered as `MComz-Foraging-UK-Europe.zim`, `-NorthAmerica`, `-Australia`, `-Pacific-Tropics`) built from pre-1930 PD texts plus US War Department *Emergency Food Plants of the Pacific*.
- **Translated collections.** Hesperian's books exist in 80+ languages; US military manuals have some translated editions. Contributors welcome.
- **Clean HTML renderings** of the heaviest-used PDFs where the source allows it. PDFs are currently bundled as downloads because their diagrams and fixed layouts don't survive automated conversion.
